Brief Bio

General

Dr. Daniel Buscombe is Research Geologist at the Grand Canyon Monitoring and Research Center, an interdisciplinary research team within the U.S. Geological Survey working on regulated rivers in the Western United States, based in Flagstaff, Arizona. His own research is in sedimentology, geomorphology, geophysics, geostatistics, and scalable computation. Previously, Buscombe was a post-doctoral fellow working at the Marine Science departments at the University of Plymouth, UK, and the University of Santa Cruz, USA. The thesis topic of his PhD (University of Plymouth, 2008) concerned sediment transport and morphodynamics of macrotidal gravel beaches. Buscombe works to encourage reproducible and open research practices across in the geoscineces, and to this end develops open-source scientific computing tools, primarily in the Python programming language.
